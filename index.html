Feeding Chart

These recommendations are only guidelines based on an average dog of normal weight and activity. We suggest 2% of the dog's weight or 1 lb for every 50 lbs of the dog. Senior dogs will vary but an average of 1.5% of the dog's bodyweight.

Puppy Feeding Recommendations

These are guidelines only and will vary with your puppy's activity level and age. Also your breeder may recommend a feeding schedule.

  • Up to 4 months puppies should get 8-10% of their body weight and feeding 4x a day
  • 4 to 6 months 6-8% of their body weight at 3x a day
  • 6 to 8 months 4-6% and a good time to start feeding 2x a day
  • 9 to 12 months 3-4%

FAQ

Is feeding a raw diet expensive?

No feeding raw food is cheaper than you think. A bigger dog of 50 lbs eating our gourmet blend is approximately $2.50 a day. A small dog is approximately $20.00 a month.

How long will raw food last in the fridge?

Raw food will last 3 to 4 days.

How do I know how much raw food to give my pet?

Raw food is based on your dogs full grown approximate weight, and we can help with that.

What are the advantages of raw food?

No chemicals, fillers, or things you have never heard of. 100% natural food. Can also help with food allergies, and it helps remove plaque and tarter from you dogs teeth.

Can we feed both kibble and raw?

No you should not. It is not recommended.

I'm ready to make the switch to raw. How do I get started?

It's best to start with a chicken or turkey mix and then later you can do beef or mix it up.

I thought chicken and turkey bones were dangerous for pets?

This is a big myth. Raw chicken bones are actually very good for your dog. The bones are soft and they are easily digestable. You should never feed your dog cooked chicken bones.

I have started the raw diet and I'm concerned my dog doesn't seem to be drinking much?

This is totally normal raw food provides food in its purest form with moisture nessecary for digesting. Kibble contains no water so dogs drink more to stay properly hydrated.
